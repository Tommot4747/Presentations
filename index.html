What to do outside of just coursework?

Internships / co-op

  • Freshman/Sophomore programs
  • Junior/Senior Internships


Applications start months in advance. Play the numbers game, be confident, and garner experience. Any step you take, brings you closer.

Learning on your own

  • Certifications & learning up to date technologies
  • Build anything you can think of --> passion projects / resume pieces
  • Personal portfolio / website

Hackathons

  • Don't be scared to take the first step, they are beginner friendly
  • Permits you to learn to cooperate in product teams and learn from others + free stuff!
  • You essentially build a product can you can even talk about in interviews

GET INVOLVED

Clubs

  • I chose my clubs due to passion an opportunity to socialize within my major
  • Academic clubs like CSS, Security, Robotics, Engineering etc..
  • I made more connections in clubs than in classes

Research

  • I did well in courses and got offered to be an undergraduate researcher by a professor
  • Various ways to get involved and can come with great benefits/experience
  • Due to my research I received a scholarship + hourly wage

The point stands, involvement is valuable and I definitely regret not getting as involved with CSS as I could have.

Computer Science vs Computer Engineering?

Computer Science Computer Eng.
Software Software + Hardware
Code + Math + Computing + Theory Code + Electronics + Firmware

My friend at Purdue is enrolled in Computer Engineering and he essentially works with a lot more C (a low level language) alongside hardware while still dipping into CS fundamentals

How heavy was the workload as a CS student?

Commute: 2 hours each day (idling on phone or homework)

Classes: 12 hours in class each week (10 week quarters)

Coursework: class dependent, can be 0 free time or a fair amount

Scrims: 4-6 hours a week + vods/matches

So as you can see, especially with a quarter system. Quite jam packed.
